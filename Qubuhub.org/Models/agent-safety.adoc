# QUBUHUB Agent Safety Policy
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2026-04-05

## Purpose
This policy defines the safety boundaries for AI systems, autonomous agents, and tool-using models operating under QUBUHUB.

The goal is not merely to make systems useful, but to ensure they remain governable under real-world conditions.

---

## 1. Safety Model
QUBUHUB treats safety as a system property, not a UI feature.

Safety must be implemented across:
- prompts,
- routing,
- memory,
- retrieval,
- tools,
- permissions,
- logging,
- output filtering,
- and deployment controls.

A system is not considered safe simply because it appears polite.

---

## 2. Disallowed Assistance
QUBUHUB systems must not materially enable or facilitate:

### 2.1 Self-Harm
- suicide facilitation,
- self-injury planning,
- concealment of self-harm,
- or optimization of self-destructive behavior.

### 2.2 Violence
- violent wrongdoing,
- physical attack planning,
- intimidation,
- or harmful weaponization guidance.

### 2.3 Exploitation and Abuse
- child sexual exploitation,
- coercion,
- predatory manipulation,
- abuse enablement,
- or non-consensual exploitation.

### 2.4 Fraud and Deception
- impersonation,
- scams,
- forged narratives,
- financial deception,
- fake authority construction,
- or social engineering abuse.

### 2.5 Cyber Abuse
- credential theft,
- malware deployment,
- unauthorized access,
- exploit chaining,
- data exfiltration,
- or operational cyber misuse.

### 2.6 Privacy Abuse
- stalking,
- doxxing,
- unlawful surveillance,
- location abuse,
- or invasive personal targeting.

### 2.7 Dangerous Scientific or Technical Misuse
- harmful biological misuse,
- dangerous chemical misuse,
- or instructions that significantly increase real-world harm capability.

---

## 3. Restricted Assistance
Some categories may be partially allowed in constrained, safety-limited form.

These include:
- cybersecurity education,
- legal information,
- medical information,
- financial education,
- dual-use technical knowledge,
- and autonomous coding workflows.

In such areas, systems should:
- remain high-level where appropriate,
- avoid operational escalation,
- and refuse clearly harmful application.

---

## 4. High-Risk Deployment Controls
Deployments with elevated risk must use stronger safeguards.

Examples include:
- agents with code execution,
- systems with database write access,
- systems with payment capability,
- agents with email or messaging authority,
- identity-linked assistants,
- and autonomous decision workflows.

Required controls may include:
- scoped permissions,
- action confirmation,
- human review,
- rate limiting,
- audit logging,
- and kill-switch capability.

---

## 5. Prompt Injection and Adversarial Resistance
QUBUHUB systems must be designed to resist adversarial misuse, including:

- hidden instruction override attempts,
- malicious retrieval poisoning,
- tool misuse redirection,
- system prompt extraction,
- and permission escalation attacks.

Untrusted content must never be treated as governing authority merely because it appears in context.

---

## 6. Refusal Standards
Refusals should:
- be firm,
- avoid unnecessary detail,
- avoid operational alternatives that preserve harmful intent,
- and avoid emotional escalation.

A refusal should not become a workaround.

---

## 7. Safety Overrides
If a user instruction conflicts with safety policy, safety policy prevails.

No user prompt, developer configuration, or downstream integration may lawfully or operationally override disallowed safety boundaries unless explicitly authorized under internal governance and legal review.

---

## 8. Monitoring and Review
QUBUHUB may monitor system performance, misuse patterns, and policy compliance to improve safety, reliability, and abuse prevention.

This includes:
- red-team review,
- incident analysis,
- abuse telemetry,
- and post-deployment policy refinement.

---

## 9. Governing Principle
If a system becomes more capable without becoming more governable, it is not ready for deployment.
